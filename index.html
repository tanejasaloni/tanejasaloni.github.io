About Me

I am a 4th year PhD candidate in Economics at the University of Southern California. My research fields are urban and environmental economics, focusing on pricing, allocation, and adaptation in developing economies.

Prior to my PhD, I was a Senior Research Associate at J-PAL South Asia, where I managed large-scale field experiments on insurance and public service delivery. I have also consulted for the World Bank on climate adaptation, and worked as a research assistant at USC analyzing the impact of education on long-run health and demographic outcomes in the United States.

I received my MA in Economics from Ashoka University, and a BA in Economics from the University of Delhi.
